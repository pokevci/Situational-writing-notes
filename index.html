Date

Subject
Body (PEEL)

Thank you for considering my request.

3. Report

Format:

Title
Introduction
Purpose
Body (PEEL)
Conclusion
Recommendations

Example:

Title: Report on the Recent School Sports Day
Introduction:
This report highlights the key events and outcomes of the School Sports Day held on 15 September 2024.
Purpose:
The purpose of this report is to assess the success of the event and its impact on student engagement.

Body:

Point: The Sports Day was a resounding success in promoting teamwork.
Evidence: Over 85% of students participated in various events, including track races and team sports.
Explanation: This encouraged students to work together, fostering a sense of community and sportsmanship. Many students formed new friendships and supported each other during the events.
Link: Such events are essential for building school spirit and encouraging physical activity.

Conclusion:

Overall, Sports Day achieved its objectives of promoting physical activity and teamwork.

Recommendations:

I suggest organizing similar events more frequently to maintain engagement among students.

10. Proposal

Format:

Title
Introduction
Purpose
Body (PEEL)
Conclusion
Recommendations
Sign-off

Example:

Title: Proposal for a School Recycling Program
Introduction:
This proposal aims to introduce a comprehensive recycling program in our school to promote environmental awareness and sustainability.
Purpose:
The purpose of this proposal is to outline the benefits of implementing a recycling program and to recommend steps for its successful implementation.

Body:

Point: Implementing a recycling program will significantly reduce waste.
Evidence: Current statistics show that schools with recycling programs reduce their landfill waste by over 50%.
Explanation: By setting up recycling bins in strategic locations, we can encourage students to recycle paper, plastic, and metal. This not only helps the environment but also fosters a culture of responsibility among students.
Link: A successful recycling program will make our school a leader in sustainability.

Point: Additionally, it will raise awareness about environmental issues.
Evidence: Educational workshops on recycling can be organized, informing students about the importance of recycling and how it impacts our planet.
Explanation: When students understand the implications of waste and learn how to recycle properly, they are more likely to adopt these practices in their daily lives.
Link: This awareness can extend beyond the school, impacting students’ families and the community.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, a recycling program will not only help reduce waste but also educate and empower our students to be responsible citizens.

Recommendations:

I recommend forming a committee to oversee the program’s implementation and organizing an awareness campaign to encourage participation.

Sign-off:

Thank you for considering this proposal.
Sincerely,
Yiming

Body Paras

1. Formal Letter

Request for Permission to Organize an Event

Number of BPs: 2–3

  • BP 1: State the event and its purpose (e.g., date, location, significance).
  • BP 2: Explain the benefits of the event to students and the community.
  • BP 3 (optional): Highlight the importance of the principal’s support.

Feedback on a Workshop

Number of BPs: 2–3

  • BP 1: Express appreciation for the workshop and state its relevance.
  • BP 2: Discuss key topics covered and their impact on students.
  • BP 3 (optional): Request follow-up sessions or future workshops.

2. Informal Letter

Invitation to a Birthday Party

Number of BPs: 2–3

  • BP 1: Express excitement and invite the friend to your birthday.
  • BP 2: Provide details of the event (date, time, activities).
  • BP 3 (optional): Share what makes the event special and hope for their attendance.

Catch Up After a Long Time

Number of BPs: 2–3

  • BP 1: Express how much you miss them and reference past experiences.
  • BP 2: Ask about their recent life events and share your own updates.
  • BP 3 (optional): Suggest meeting up to reconnect.

3. Report

School Event Report

Number of BPs: 3–4

  • BP 1: Introduction to the event with objectives.
  • BP 2: Key findings (e.g., attendance and participation rates).
  • BP 3: Analysis of the findings and feedback from attendees.
  • BP 4 (optional): Suggestions for future improvements.

Survey Results Report

Number of BPs: 3–4

  • BP 1: Summary of survey findings.
  • BP 2: Key statistics and trends.
  • BP 3: Analysis of the implications of the results.
  • BP 4 (optional): Recommendations based on the findings.

4. Article

Importance of Extracurricular Activities

Number of BPs: 3–4

  • BP 1: Introduce the topic and hook the reader's attention.
  • BP 2: Discuss the benefits of extracurricular activities with supporting evidence.
  • BP 3: Provide personal anecdotes or examples to support your argument.
  • BP 4 (optional): Conclusion with a call to action for students to get involved.

Review of a Book or Movie

Number of BPs: 3–4

  • BP 1: Provide a brief summary of the book or movie.
  • BP 2: Discuss key themes or standout moments with evidence.
  • BP 3: Share personal reflections on the impact of the book or movie.
  • BP 4 (optional): Conclusion with a recommendation for readers.

5. Speech

Awareness on Environmental Issues

Number of BPs: 3–5

  • BP 1: Start with a strong opening to engage the audience.
  • BP 2: Discuss the importance of protecting the environment with current statistics.
  • BP 3: Share a personal story related to environmental action.
  • BP 4: Address challenges or counterarguments.
  • BP 5 (optional): Conclude with a call to action for the audience.

Motivational Speech for Students

Number of BPs: 3–5

  • BP 1: Start with a strong opening to inspire students.
  • BP 2: Share success stories of famous individuals and their perseverance.
  • BP 3: Relate these stories to the students' own experiences.
  • BP 4: Address common challenges students face.
  • BP 5 (optional): End with an inspiring message for students to pursue their goals.

6. Email

Request for Class Notes

Number of BPs: 2–3

  • BP 1: State the purpose of the email (request for notes due to absence).
  • BP 2: Provide details on which classes were missed and why.
  • BP 3 (optional): Thank them in advance for their help.

Thank You Email After an Interview

Number of BPs: 2–3

  • BP 1: Express thanks for the opportunity and reference the interview.
  • BP 2: Mention specific topics discussed that stood out to you.
  • BP 3 (optional): Reiterate your interest in the position and hope to hear from them soon.

7. Review

Review of a Local Restaurant

Number of BPs: 3–4

  • BP 1: Brief overview of the restaurant experience.
  • BP 2: Discuss specific dishes ordered and their quality.
  • BP 3: Provide your personal reflections on the atmosphere and service.
  • BP 4 (optional): Give an overall recommendation based on your experience.

Review of a Concert

Number of BPs: 3–4

  • BP 1: Summarize the concert experience.
  • BP 2: Highlight memorable performances or moments.
  • BP 3: Discuss the crowd and overall atmosphere.
  • BP 4 (optional): Conclude with your recommendation on whether others should attend.
